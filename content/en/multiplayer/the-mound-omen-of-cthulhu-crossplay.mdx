<Section icon={Shield} title="Sanity Mechanics in Co-op Play">

The most unique aspect of **the mound omen of cthulhu crossplay** is how the sanity system interacts with multiplayer. Unlike other horror games where everyone sees the same jump scares, The Mound uses "Perception Distortion." This means two players standing in the same spot might see completely different environments.

As sanity drops, a player might see a teammate as a shambling monster. Without clear communication, this often leads to "friendly fire" incidents. The spatial voice chat reflects this; if a teammate's sanity is low, their voice might sound distant, distorted, or replaced by whispers, making it harder to coordinate.

**Common Sanity Distortions in Multiplayer:**
- **False Horns**: One player hears the extraction horn while the rest of the squad hears silence.
- **Phantom Teammates**: Seeing a fifth "explorer" following the group who isn't actually there.
- **Blood Rain**: A localized weather effect that obscures the vision of only one player.
- **Terrain Shifting**: A path that looks safe to the Navigator might appear as a pit of tentacles to a fractured Scout.

<Callout type="warning" title="Reality Check Protocol">
When you see a threat, always use the "Confirm Target" callout. If your teammates do not see the same creature, it is likely a hallucination. Do not waste precious ammunition on shadows.
</Callout>

</Section>
