<Section icon={Shield} title="Solo Survival Mechanics and Noise Control">

In *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu*, the environment reacts to your presence. Loud noises, such as sprinting through brush or firing a musket, attract the attention of Y'm-bhi sentries and other undead horrors. For a solo player, being swarmed is often a death sentence because you lack the fire rate to suppress multiple angles simultaneously.

| Mechanic | Solo Impact | Team Impact | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Noise Level** | **Critical** / Attracts enemies to your lone position | **Moderate** / Shared aggro across 4 players | Use crouch-walking near ruins |
| **Ox Cart** | **Manual** / Must defend and move it alone | **Distributed** / One player guards, others loot | Deposit loot every 5 minutes |
| **Sanity** | **Fatal** / No one to reality-check hallucinations | **Manageable** / Squad can confirm threats | Retreat if the screen turns red |
| **Revives** | **None** / Death results in immediate failure | **High** / Teammates can revive fallen players | Carry extra healing poultices |

The ox cart is your lifeline. It stores your treasure and spare equipment. However, as a solo player, you cannot leave it unattended for long. If you wander too far into a ruin, the jungle may reclaim the cart or spawn enemies to ambush you upon your return.

<Callout type="warning" title="Firearm Risks">
Firing a flintlock musket is not just a combat choice; it is a beacon. The sound can be heard from vast distances in the jungle. Only fire if you have a clear path to retreat or if the target is a high-threat Y'm-bhi that has already spotted you.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Eye} title="Managing Sanity and Hallucinations Alone">

The most terrifying aspect of playing solo is the Sanity System. In co-op, you can ask a friend, "Do you see that monster by the tree?" In solo, if you see a monster, you have to decide if it's a physical threat or a hallucination. Wasting your limited ammunition on a phantom can leave you defenseless when a real Y'm-bhi attacks.

**Common Solo Hallucinations:**
- **False Horns:** Hearing the ox cart's horn when it hasn't moved.
- **Shadow Figures:** Seeing teammates that aren't there, leading you into traps.
- **Environmental Shifts:** The ground appearing to pulse or change color.
- **Blood Rain:** Localized weather effects that obscure vision but aren't "real."

Without a teammate to guide you, you must become your own anchor. If you feel your sanity slipping—indicated by the edges of the screen darkening or whispers becoming louder—immediately stop looting and head toward your extraction point. The risk of a "Squad Wipe" (even as a solo) increases exponentially as your perception fractures.

<Callout type="info" title="Crucifix Utility">
The crucifix is not just for flavor; it is a diagnostic tool. Using it on a creature will reveal its stress level. If the creature does not react to the crucifix, there is a high probability it is a hallucination.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Solo Extraction Loop">

To succeed in **the mound omen of cthulhu solo** mode, you must follow a disciplined loop. Greed is the primary cause of failed expeditions. Knowing when to leave with a partial haul is better than losing everything to the jungle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Contract & Loadout">
    Select a contract on the galleon. For solo, prioritize "Resource Recovery" over "Bounty Hunting." Equip a bow, a knife, and plenty of water.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Shoreline Infiltration">
    Enter the jungle slowly. Move the ox cart to a central, hidden location near the beach. Do not drag it into dense vegetation where it can be easily trapped.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stealth Looting">
    Scout ruins using your bow to clear sentries. Collect the required treasure value first. If you find a fort logbook, prioritize it, as it unlocks new spawns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Sanity Maintenance">
    Every 10 minutes, return to the cart and rest briefly. If the environment starts to look "wrong," stop searching and prepare for extraction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Safe Extraction">
    Once the contract is met, return the cart to the galleon. Do not take "one last look" at a nearby ruin; the jungle's aggression scales with time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Resource | Priority | Solo Value | Extraction Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Contract Treasure** | **High** | Required for progression | Load into cart immediately |
| **Fort Logbooks** | **High** | Unlocks new map points | Keep in inventory (don't drop) |
| **Cursed Relics** | **Medium** | High gold / High sanity drain | Only take if sanity is > 80% |
| **Meat/Water** | **Medium** | Essential for survival | Process on ship after raid |

<Callout type="tip" title="Fort Progression">
Finding and interacting with logbooks in abandoned forts is the best way to make solo play easier over time. Unlocking a starting point closer to the high-value ruins reduces the time you spend exposed in the open jungle.
</Callout>
