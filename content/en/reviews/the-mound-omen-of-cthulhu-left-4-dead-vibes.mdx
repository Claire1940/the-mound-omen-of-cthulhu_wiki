<Section icon={HeartPulse} title="The Sanity and Hallucination System">

The most innovative feature in The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu is its complete lack of a sanity HUD. There are no meters, warning sirens, or status icons to tell you that your character is losing their mind. Instead, madness manifests as private visual and auditory hallucinations. What you see and hear may be completely different from what your teammates experience.

As your sanity slips, the jungle begins to distort. You might see the ground pulse with red veins, witness localized blood rain, or hear the extraction horn blowing in the distance when the cart is actually stationary. 

| Hallucination Type | Danger Level | Visual / Audio Cue | Squad Countermeasure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bleeding Jungle** | Low | Red environmental shift, fake rain | Ask teammates to confirm if they see the color shift |
| **False Horn Calls** | Medium | Distant extraction horn audio | Check the cart's physical location before moving |
| **False Teammates** | High | Friendly silhouettes in the fog | Use spatial voice chat to verify player positions |
| **Teammate Monsters** | Extreme | Allies look like undead creatures | Use voice confirmation before taking a panic shot |
| **Shifting Terrain** | Medium | Fake paths, illusory treasure | Let the player with the highest sanity lead the way |

<Callout type="success" title="The Reality-Check Protocol">
Establish a simple communication rule with your team. When you spot an unexpected item, path, or teammate silhouette in the fog, ask: *"Do you see this?"* If your team answers no, ignore the illusion and stay close to the group.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Expedition Loop">

Every successful expedition requires disciplined execution. Following a structured loop ensures you secure enough treasure to satisfy the captain's contract without losing your squad to the escalating horrors of the jungle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose the Contract">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Divide the Shared Gear">
    Distribute your limited weapons, oil lamps, and ammunition across the squad. Ensure no single player carries all the light sources or firearms.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Escort the Ox Cart">
    Move inland while keeping the ox cart central to your formation. Use the cart to deposit heavy treasures and store spare ammunition as you explore ruins.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Quiet Exploration">
    Move slowly and avoid unnecessary noise. Cut vegetation sparingly, walk instead of sprint, and use silent weapons like bows to avoid triggering horde waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Verify Shifting Threat Levels">
    Watch for signs of environmental decay, such as thickening fog and aggressive undead behavior. Use the reality-check protocol to manage sanity slips.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Extract Safely">
    Once the contract's value is met, return to the galleon. Do not let greed push you into deeper, decayed ruins when your ammunition and sanity are depleted.
  </Step>
</Steps>
