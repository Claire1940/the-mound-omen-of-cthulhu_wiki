<Section icon={Shield} title="Co-op and Solo Matchmaking Protocols">

The game supports solo play alongside online co-op for up to four players. However, playing alone significantly increases the difficulty because there are no teammates to protect the treasure-carrying ox cart or help you verify sanity-warping hallucinations. When forming a squad through the Discord community, establishing clear roles before launching from the *Tempestad* galleon is essential.

| Squad Role | Primary Equipment | Core Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Navigator** | Oil Lamp, Bow | Tracks routes, secures logbooks, and guides the team through fog. |
| **Cart Defender** | Flintlock Musket, Knife | Remains near the ox cart, covers the rear, and handles close threats. |
| **Scout / Gatherer** | Bow, Treasure Medallion | Locates hidden Tulu metal, gathers meat/water, and triggers items. |
| **Heavy Support** | Matchlock Musket, Knife | Eliminates high-threat targets and secures defensive perimeters. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Do not concentrate all critical utility items on a single player. If your only lamp carrier or navigator is incapacitated or separated, the remaining squad will quickly succumb to darkness and panic.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Managing Sanity and Communication in Co-op">

The most unique challenge in *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu* is the complete absence of a traditional sanity HUD. You will not see a meter indicating your mental decay. Instead, madness manifests as private visual and auditory hallucinations that differ for every player in the squad.

To counter these psychological traps, you must use structured communication. Discord voice channels provide a stable alternative when in-game spatial audio becomes distorted by spreading corruption.
