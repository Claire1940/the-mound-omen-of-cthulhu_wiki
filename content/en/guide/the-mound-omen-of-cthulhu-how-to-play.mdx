<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the Core Extraction Loop">

The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu introduces a unique spin on the traditional extraction genre. Instead of keeping the physical weapons, armor, and trinkets you find during a raid, all extracted loot is automatically converted into value-based rewards at the end of the run. This system shifts the focus from hoarding specific gear pieces to maximizing the total financial and material value of your inventory before escaping. 

Your starting loadout is determined entirely by the contract you choose before deploying. Harder contracts grant superior equipment, but they also place you in significantly more dangerous environments where the risk of losing everything is high. Because there is no traditional skill tree, progression relies on your tactical execution, map knowledge, and team coordination.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Managing Madness and Hallucinations">

Madness is a constant threat in the dark jungles. As your madness level rises, you will begin to experience severe visual and auditory distortions. A crucial mechanic to note is that these hallucinations are entirely client-side. What you see is not shared with your teammates. For example, you might see duplicate enemies or observe a teammate morphing into a giant beast, while your partner sees a completely normal environment.

To prevent tragic friendly fire incidents, constant communication is vital. You must verify targets verbally before pulling the trigger. Fortunately, madness can be managed and lowered by staying in close proximity to your allies. Standing near the ox cart provides an even stronger sanity restoration effect, making it the central anchor point for your team during exploration.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Essential Equipment and Utility Items">

Before departing from your ship, you can purchase utility items that significantly increase your chances of survival. These tools provide defensive options, navigation assistance, and utility that can save your team during chaotic encounters. Understanding when and how to deploy these tools is a fundamental part of mastering the game.

The crucifix is a powerful defensive tool that slows down approaching enemies, buying your team precious time to reposition or heal. The map is essential for navigating the dense, confusing jungle pathways. For lighting, you have access to both torches and lamps. The torch is primarily used to burn away grabbing vines that attempt to trap you, while the lamp serves as a versatile light source that can also be thrown to ignite areas or damage enemies.

| Item Name | Primary Function | Tactical Application |
| Crucifix | Slows enemy movement | Creating escape windows during combat |
| Map | Navigation and planning | Mapping out safe paths to extraction |
| Torch | Light and vine defense | Burning away environmental hazards |
| Lamp | Light and throwable weapon | Illuminating dark areas or throwing to damage foes |

<Callout type="tip" title="Utility Tip">
Keep a lamp in your inventory even if you have a torch. Being able to throw a lamp to light up a distant area or trigger an explosive hazard is highly valuable.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Noise Control and Stealth Mechanics">

Sound is one of the primary ways enemies locate your team. Almost every action you take generates noise, and louder actions will pull hostiles from a wider radius. Running in heavy armor, scaring flocks of birds, breaking dry branches, and firing unsilenced weapons are all guaranteed ways to invite unwanted attention.

To survive, you must balance speed with stealth. Moving slowly and avoiding environmental noise triggers will allow you to slip past dangerous encounters. If you must fight, try to do so quickly and move away from the area immediately, as the sound of combat will likely draw nearby monsters to your last known location.

| Action Type | Noise Level | Enemy Attraction Range |
| Crouching | Negligible | Extremely short |
| Walking | Low | Short |
| Running (Heavy Armor) | High | Medium to Long |
| Gunfire / Branch Snapping | Extreme | Very Long |

<Callout type="info" title="Stealth Advice">
Pay attention to the ground. Avoid stepping on dry branches and keep an eye out for roosting birds that will fly away and screech if you get too close.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression and Ship Upgrades">

While the game lacks a traditional character skill tree, you can permanently upgrade your survival capabilities on your ship. These upgrades directly impact your healing potential and resource management during runs, making them a priority for long-term progression.

To upgrade your healing rations, navigate two levels below the main deck of your ship. Upgrading your rations increases the amount of health restored per use, which is vital for surviving the high-damage attacks encountered in harder contracts. Additionally, hunting deer during your runs provides bonus points that accelerate your access to these upgrades.
