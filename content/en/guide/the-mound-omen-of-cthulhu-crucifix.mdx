<Section icon={Shield} title="Sanity Management & Noise Control">

The deep jungle is powered by a complex madness system that actively attempts to sabotage your expedition. Prolonged exposure to darkness, isolation, and supernatural phenomena will cause players to experience severe hallucinations, disguised traps, and visual distortions.

| Hazard | Sanity Effect | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Isolation** | Rapid sanity loss, muffled audio, grey screen | Stay close to teammates at all times |
| **Black Ravens** | Smoke cloud triggers massive madness spike | Shoot immediately upon sight or sound |
| **Madness Shrines** | Whispers build up, causing severe hallucinations | Avoid standing near or interacting with them |
| **Jungle Darkness** | Gradual sanity decline over time | Stay near the glowing cart or eat mushrooms |

To survive the psychological toll, players must balance their positioning and monitor environmental triggers. Making excessive noise—such as firing muskets, snapping tree branches, or overusing the medallion—will accelerate the jungle's awakening and spawn deadlier threats.

Your starting loadout is sparse, forcing you to make the most of found gear and specialized tools. Understanding which weapons provide the best utility and damage output is essential for clearing out Lovecraftian horrors.

| Weapon / Tool | Combat Style | Verdict |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throwing Axe** | Ranged projectile, highly reusable | **Excellent** (Throw and retrieve loop) |
| **Spear** | Long-reach poke attacks | **Good** (Safe distance melee damage) |
| **Pole Hammer** | Slow, heavy-impact strikes | **Good** (High stagger potential) |
| **Firearms** | High burst damage at range | **Situational** (Extremely loud, attracts hordes) |

While firearms like the musket offer immense stopping power, they should only be used as a last resort during extraction. For general combat, silent throwing weapons and long-reach melee options keep the jungle quiet while dealing consistent damage.

Additionally, utility tools serve dual purposes. The standard lantern can be thrown like a grenade to inflict massive damage on the creeping canopy, which is widely considered one of the most annoying creatures in the game. Meanwhile, the torch is vital for stopping the resurrection cycle of fallen enemies.

<Callout type="tip" title="Stopping Zombie Revives">
When a zombie falls, nearby cursed vines will attempt to revive it. Wave your torch directly over the fallen corpse to force the vines to retreat, ensuring the enemy stays down permanently.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Expedition Guide">

Every expedition starts from the safety of your galleon and takes you deep into hostile, uncharted territories. Follow these structured steps to ensure your crew returns with treasures and lives intact.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Galleon Preparation">
    Gather your fellow explorers on the galleon. Sign a contract with the captain to secure your starting gear, including muskets, crossbows, swords, oil lamps, and reviving salts. Split the equipment evenly among the crew before boarding the rowboat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Jungle Navigation">
    Once on the mainland, move as a cohesive unit. Locate the supply cart and utilize the proximity voice chat to coordinate movements. If you ever get separated, blow the horn to call the cart to your position, but be mindful of the noise it generates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Resource Gathering">
    Focus on fulfilling the captain's contract by gathering food, rescuing lost survivors, and collecting ancient treasures. Hunt wild deer whenever possible to provide the ship's cook with ingredients to permanently upgrade your squad's rations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Rowboat Extraction">
    You do not need to wait for the cart to escape, but all living team members must be present at the rowboat to extract. If a teammate is snatched by a prowler late in the run, it is often safer to wait at the extraction point rather than risking the entire squad.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Cutting Your Losses">
You do not fail the expedition if you fail to meet the captain's contract. While you will receive less experience and tokens, escaping with a recovered logbook, a special artifact, or a rescued survivor is always better than dying in the jungle.
