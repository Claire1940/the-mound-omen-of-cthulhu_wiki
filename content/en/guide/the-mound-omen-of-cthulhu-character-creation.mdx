<Section icon={Map} title="Character System and Archetype Overview">

The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu departs from traditional RPG mechanics by offering a set roster of established characters. Instead of a blank-slate avatar, players step into the shoes of Spanish explorers, each with their own history and motivations. This design choice allows the developers at ACE Team to craft a more atmospheric narrative, where the psychological horror and Lovecraftian dread feel personal to the survivors.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Visual Customization and Historical Authenticity">

The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu takes a unique approach to character visuals. Instead of a slider-based creation system, the developers worked with real costume designers to create physical replicas of 16th-century Spanish attire. These garments were then photo-scanned to create highly detailed 3D models. This ensures that every player looks like an authentic conquistador, maintaining the game's gritty, grounded aesthetic.

**Visual Customization Elements:**
- **Armor Sets**: Different types of leather and metal breastplates found in abandoned forts.
- **Headgear**: Helmets and hats that provide both protection and a distinct silhouette.
- **Weapon Display**: Machetes, flintlock pistols, and rifles are visibly holstered on the character model.
- **Environmental Wear**: Mud, blood, and moisture accumulate on clothing during the expedition.

| Customization Category | How to Change | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clothing/Uniform** | Found in mainland / Rescued characters | Purely Visual / Historical |
| **Primary Weapon** | Selected at Captain's Contract | Combat Range and Power |
| **Utility Gear** | Inventory slots (Oil lamps, Gunpowder) | Visibility and Utility |
| **Sanity Effects** | Exposure to Eldritch influence | Visual distortion / Hallucinations |

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Unlock New Characters">

While you start with a limited selection, the world of *The Mound* is filled with lost souls from previous failed expeditions. Rescuing these survivors is a primary way to expand your roster and access new character perspectives. This "rescue" mechanic adds a layer of progression to the character system that traditional creation screens lack.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Accept the Rescue Contract">
    Visit the Captain on the Tempest Dot and look for contracts involving "Marooned Survivors" or "Lost Expedition Members."
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Survivor">
    Navigate to abandoned forts or indigenous camps on the mainland. Listen for audio cues, as survivors may call out for help.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secure the Area">
    Survivors are often guarded by indigenous stragglers or Eldritch stalkers. You must eliminate these threats before the rescue can proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Escort to Extraction">
    Guide the survivor back to your extraction point. They may be weak or injured, requiring your team to provide cover.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Finalize the Unlock">
    Once successfully extracted, the new character becomes available in your roster for future runs, bringing their own unique lore and visuals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Rescue Type | Difficulty | Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Expedition Straggler** | Low | New Basic Archetype |
| **Marooned Officer** | Medium | Rare Gear + New Character |
| **Failed Explorer** | High | Specialized Lore + Unique Visuals |

<Callout type="info" title="Roster Expansion">
Rescued characters are not just "skins." They are integrated into the story progression, and their presence on the Tempest Dot can unlock new dialogue options with the ship's cook or the Captain.
</Callout>

</Section>
