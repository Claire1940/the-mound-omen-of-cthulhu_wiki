<Section icon={Target} title="Firearms Breakdown: Muskets and Gunpowder">

Firearms in the expedition are high-risk, high-reward tools. The **the mound omen of cthulhu weapons** system emphasizes the "one-shot" nature of 17th-century combat. Once you fire, you are vulnerable for several seconds while performing a complex reloading animation. 

The distinction between Matchlock and Flintlock is critical for survival. Matchlocks require a burning cord, making them difficult to use in damp environments, whereas Flintlocks use a piece of flint to ignite the priming powder, offering slightly better reliability but still failing in downpours.

| Feature | Flintlock Musket | Matchlock Musket |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Damage** | Very High | Extreme |
| **Reload Speed** | Moderate (8-10s) | Slow (12-15s) |
| **Noise Level** | Extreme | Extreme |
| **Rain Resistance** | Low | None |
| **Best Use** | Elite enemies | Boss encounters |

<Callout type="warning" title="Ammo Conservation">
Ammunition and gunpowder are shared resources provided by the Captain. Do not waste shots on silhouettes in the fog; verify the target with your squad first to ensure you aren't shooting at a hallucination.
</Callout>

Firearms also serve a psychological role. The loud report of a musket can be used as a signal to distant teammates, but it simultaneously "wakes" the jungle. As noise levels rise, the environment shifts toward a "Red Moon" state, increasing the aggression and spawn rate of nearby threats.

<Section icon={Map} title="Utility Gear and Expedition Tools">

Survival is about more than just killing monsters. Specialized utility gear allows the squad to locate treasure and maintain sanity. - **Oil Lamp:** Provides a radius of safety against shadows.

- **Crucifix:** Used to read the stress levels of Y'm-bhi sentries.
- **Ox Cart:** Not a weapon, but your mobile armory and loot storage.
