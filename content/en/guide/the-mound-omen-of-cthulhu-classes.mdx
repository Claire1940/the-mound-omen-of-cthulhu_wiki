In the haunting world of *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu*, the concept of a "class" differs significantly from standard hero shooters or MMOs. <Section icon={Target} title="Weapon-Based Role Guide">

Because **the mound omen of cthulhu classes** are defined by equipment, understanding the weapon meta is crucial. The game features a variety of 16th-century armaments, ranging from silent bows to loud, powerful firearms. Each choice carries a heavy trade-off, particularly concerning the "Noise" mechanic, which can alert the jungle to your presence.

| Role | Core Weapon | Secondary Item | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scout** | Bow / Crossbow | Oil Lamp | Silent, reusable ammo | Low burst damage |
| **Vanguard** | Machete / Axe | Shield | High durability, no ammo | Risky close combat |
| **Marksman** | Flintlock Pistol | Gunpowder | High damage, range | Loud, fails in rain |
| **Support** | Sailor's Knife | Food / Meds | High utility | Weak in direct fights |

The environment plays a massive role in how these "classes" perform. For instance, if the weather shifts to heavy rain, matchlock and flintlock firearms will malfunction. In these scenarios, the "Marksman" role becomes effectively useless, forcing the player to switch to melee or thrown weapons like axes.

<Callout type="warning" title="Noise Management">
Guns are powerful but dangerous. A single shot can "wake the forest," causing a gauntlet of Eldritch enemies to converge on your location. Use firearms only as a last resort or when you are prepared for a major confrontation.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Expedition Preparation: The Pre-Run Ritual">

Success in *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu* begins on the *Tempest Dot*. Before you set foot in the jungle, you must coordinate with your squad and the Ship's Captain to finalize your loadout. Since resources are scarce, you cannot afford to waste inventory slots on redundant items.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Sign the Contract">
    Speak with the Captain to accept a pre-run contract. These objectives define your goals, such as finding specific artifacts or rescuing survivors, and provide the initial gear for the run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Distribute the Armory">
    The Captain provides a collective pool of weapons and gunpowder. You must decide as a team who takes the rifle and who takes the knife. You cannot all have the best gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Consult the Cook">
    Speak with the ship's cook to determine which foodstuffs are best to retrieve. Food is essential for maintaining stamina and health during the grueling trek toward the Mound.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize the Oxcart">
    Ensure your oxcart is ready. This automated wagon follows your progress and serves as your primary stash for high-value loot that won't fit in your personal 6-slot inventory.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Shared Progression">
Don't worry about "stealing" XP. Progression and story unlocks are shared across the team. Focus on the mission objectives rather than individual loot hoarding to advance the narrative for everyone.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Impact of Madness and Sanity">

A unique aspect of **the mound omen of cthulhu classes** is how they interact with the game's psychological horror elements. As you delve deeper into the jungle, your sanity will begin to fray. This isn't just a status bar; it actively changes your perception of reality, making the "Support" and "Communication" roles vital.

**Sanity Effects Include:**
- **Auditory Hallucinations**: Hearing footsteps or screams that aren't there.
- **Visual Distortions**: Seeing "phantom" loot over spike pits or shifting colors in the environment.
- **Paranoia**: Seeing your teammates as hostile monsters or seeing "fake" allies that are actually enemies in disguise.
- **Sensory Illusions**: Flickering lamps or disappearing structures that confuse navigation.
