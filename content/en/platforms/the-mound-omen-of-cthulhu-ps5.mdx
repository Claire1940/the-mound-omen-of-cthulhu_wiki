<Section icon={Target} title="The Core Expedition Loop: Steps to Survival">

Every mission in **the mound omen of cthulhu ps5** follows a structured loop that begins aboard the *Tempestad* galleon. Success is not measured by how many monsters you kill, but by how much treasure you successfully extract. Greed is the primary cause of squad wipes, as the jungle becomes exponentially more dangerous the longer you stay.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Contract Selection">
    Speak with the Captain to select a contract. Each contract specifies a minimum treasure value or a specific relic that must be recovered. Review the destination's difficulty and weather forecast before committing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Role Distribution">
    The Captain provides shared equipment. Do not let one player carry all the ammunition or both lamps. Assign a Navigator, a Cart Defender, and two Combat Scouts to ensure all bases are covered.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Jungle Infiltration">
    Move toward the target area while escorting the ox cart. The cart is your only mobile storage for heavy treasure. Keep the path clear of debris and avoid running to minimize noise levels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Treasure Recovery">
    Use the Treasure-Finding Medallion to locate Tulu-metal. Be warned: striking the medallion creates a loud metallic clang that attracts nearby Y'm-bhi sentries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Safe Extraction">
    Once the contract goal is met, return to the shoreline. If the environment begins to pulse red or fog thickens rapidly, abandon optional looting and extract immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Ox Cart Management">
The ox cart can become stuck in dense vegetation or blocked by enemies. Always keep at least one player with a machete or knife near the front of the cart to maintain momentum.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering the Sanity System">

The most innovative feature of **the mound omen of cthulhu ps5** is the lack of a traditional sanity meter. Instead of a HUD bar, your character's mental state is reflected through private hallucinations. Because these effects are client-side, your teammates will not see what you see.

**Common Hallucination Types:**
- **False Horns**: You hear the extraction signal, but the cart hasn't moved.
- **Teammate Mimics**: You see a teammate walking into the woods, but they are actually standing right behind you.
- **Blood Rain**: The screen tints red and visibility drops, though healthy players see clear skies.
- **Phantom Enemies**: Shapes in the fog that disappear when shot, wasting your precious ammunition.

| Hallucination | Detection Method | Squad Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mimic Silhouette** | Voice Check | Ask player to jump or crouch |
| **False Sound** | Directional Audio | Compare location with Navigator's map |
| **Terrain Shift** | Physical Collision | Have a "sane" player lead the path |
| **Red Tint** | Visual Confirmation | Confirm weather state via voice chat |

<Callout type="warning" title="Friendly Fire">
Severe madness can make teammates look like undead monsters. Never fire at a silhouette in the fog without receiving a verbal confirmation through spatial voice chat first.
</Callout>

</Section>

**Notable Enemies:**
- **Y'm-bhi Sentries**: These guardians watch you from the treeline. They build stress and sanity loss just by staring. Use a crucifix to check their "stress level" before they charge.
- **Undead Wanderers**: Common threats that roam the beaches. They are slow but can overwhelm a squad if noise levels attract a swarm.
- **Corrupted Explorers**: If a player dies and isn't revived, the jungle reanimates them. They retain some player-like movements, making them unpredictable.
