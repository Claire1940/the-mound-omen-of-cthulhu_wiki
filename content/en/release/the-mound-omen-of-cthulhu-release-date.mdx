<Section icon={Target} title="Expedition Loop: Beginner Setup Guide">

Survival in the cursed jungle requires more than just firepower; it requires a disciplined understanding of the extraction loop. Every mission begins aboard the **Tempestad galleon**, where the crew must coordinate their roles before setting foot on the shore. Greed is the primary cause of squad wipes, as the jungle grows more hostile the longer you remain.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Contract Selection">
    Speak with the captain on the galleon to select a contract. These objectives range from recovering specific relics to securing a minimum total treasure value. **Always check the required loot value** before departing, as you cannot extract successfully without meeting this quota.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Loadout Distribution">
    The captain provides shared equipment. Do not concentrate all firearms or lamps on one player. Assign a dedicated **Scout** (bow/stealth), a **Defender** (firearms/melee), and a **Navigator** (lamp/map) to ensure the squad can handle diverse threats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Ox Cart Escort">
    The ox cart is your mobile vault. Deposit valuables regularly as you move. It follows the group, but its metallic horn can be imitated by hallucinations. **Always verify the cart's position** with teammates before following the sound of a horn into the fog.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Noise and Stealth Management">
    Running, firing guns, and cutting vegetation attract the jungle's attention. Use bows or knives for silent eliminations. Save your flintlock muskets for emergency stopping power against larger supernatural threats or when the squad is cornered.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Extraction Decision">
    Once the contract is met, the environment will begin to distort (reddening skies, thicker fog). Decide as a team whether to push for optional treasure or retreat. **Retreating with a completed contract** is always better than losing everything to a late-game ambush.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Weather Hazards">
Rain is a lethal threat to firearm users. Flintlock and matchlock weapons depend on dry gunpowder and **will fail to fire during heavy rain**. Always ensure at least two team members are equipped with bows or knives to maintain combat readiness in wet conditions.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Brain} title="Sanity and Hallucination Mechanics">

The most innovative feature of the game is the lack of a traditional Sanity HUD. You won't see a bar telling you how "mad" you are. Instead, the game uses **private perception distortion**. One player might see a teammate as a shambling undead, while another sees the jungle floor bleeding.

**Reality Check Protocol:**
- **Voice Confirmation**: Always ask "Do you see that?" before firing at a silhouette in the fog.
- **Spatial Positioning**: If a teammate's voice sounds like it's coming from the left, but their silhouette is on the right, the silhouette is likely a hallucination.
- **Stay Grouped**: Madness escalates rapidly when an explorer is separated from the spatial voice chat range of the squad.
