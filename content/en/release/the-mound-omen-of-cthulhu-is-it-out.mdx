<Section icon={Map} title="Beginner Guide: Your First Expedition">

Every successful journey in The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu begins aboard the **Tempestad galleon**. This hub serves as your preparation area where the squad selects contracts and manages resources. The core loop involves entering the jungle, filling an ox cart with treasure, and returning to the ship before the environment becomes too hostile.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select a Captain's Contract">
    Visit the Captain on the galleon to choose your objective. Contracts vary from finding specific relics to reaching a total treasure value. **Check the required loot value** before departing to ensure you don't extract too early.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Distribute Squad Equipment">
    The Captain provides shared tools. Do not hoard all items on one player. Assign one person the **Oil Lamp** for visibility, one the **Bow** for silent kills, and another the **Treasure-finding Medallion**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Escort the Ox Cart">
    The ox cart is your lifeline and mobile storage. It follows the group automatically but can become stuck or targeted by enemies. Always keep the path clear and **deposit valuables immediately** after finding them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Noise and Weather">
    Running and firing muskets attracts **Undead Wanderers**. If it begins to rain, your gunpowder weapons may fail. Switch to **knives or bows** during storms to maintain combat effectiveness without the risk of misfires.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Extract Safely">
    Once the contract goal is met, the squad must decide whether to push deeper for optional loot or return. If visibility drops or **red vegetation** starts pulsing, it is time to leave.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Greed Kills">
Losing your entire squad results in the loss of all collected treasure for that run. It is always better to extract with a partial haul than to lose everything to a late-game hallucination.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Sanity and Perception Mechanics">

The most innovative feature of The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu is its **hidden sanity system**. There is no sanity bar or meter on the HUD. Instead, the game uses environmental cues and private hallucinations to signal a player's deteriorating mental state. This forces players to communicate constantly to verify what is real.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Visual Distortions">
    - **Bleeding Jungle**: The environment turns deep red and appears to pulse.
    - **False Silhouettes**: You may see a teammate standing in the fog when they are actually behind you.
    - **Shifting Terrain**: Safe paths may appear as traps, or vice versa.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auditory Hallucinations">
    - **Phantom Horns**: Hearing the extraction signal when no one has triggered it.
    - **Mimicked Voices**: Hearing a teammate call for help from a direction they aren't in.
    - **Whispers**: Increasing background noise that masks the footsteps of actual enemies.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To counter these effects, squads should use **Spatialized Voice Chat**. If you see a threat, call it out. If your teammates cannot see it, you are likely hallucinating. Assigning a "Lead Navigator" who is currently the most stable (experiencing the fewest distortions) is a common high-level tactic.

<Callout type="tip" title="Reality Check">
Use a **Crucifix** to test the environment. If an enemy doesn't react to the light of the crucifix or doesn't show "stress" signs, it might not be a physical threat.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Weapons and Loadouts">

In the 17th-century setting, combat is slow, deliberate, and dangerous. Reloading a musket takes several seconds, making every shot count. Understanding the strengths and weaknesses of your gear is essential for surviving the **Y'm-bhi Sentries** and other jungle horrors.

| Weapon Type | Primary Strength | Major Weakness | Recommended Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bow** | **Silent, Rain-proof** | Lower stopping power | Stealth / Scout |
| **Flintlock Musket** | **High Burst Damage** | Loud, Slow reload | Heavy Combat |
| **Matchlock Musket** | Long range | **Useless in Rain** | Sniper / Support |
| **Knife** | No ammo required | **Dangerous Proximity** | Backup / Finishing |
| **Oil Lamp** | Essential Visibility | Attracts attention | Navigator |

A balanced 4-player team should distribute these roles to ensure they can handle any situation. One player should always focus on the bow to pick off sentries without alerting the entire zone, while another keeps a musket ready for larger supernatural threats.

<Callout type="success" title="Loadout Tip">
The **Treasure-finding Medallion** is powerful but loud. Only use it when the area is secured, as the metallic "clang" can be heard by enemies from a significant distance.
</Callout>
