<Section icon={Target} title="Sanity and Hallucination Mechanics">

The game features no traditional HUD, health bars, or sanity meters. Psychological decay manifests directly through altered environmental rendering and auditory illusions that target players individually.

| Hallucination Type | Visual / Auditory Effect | Team Danger | Correct Countermeasure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bleeding Jungle** | Red rain, pulsing flora | Distorted navigation paths | Verify weather with teammates |
| **False Horn Calls** | Distant extraction signals | Lures players into ambushes | Confirm cart location on map |
| **Monster Illusions** | Teammates look like beasts | High risk of friendly fire | Use voice chat before shooting |
| **False Silhouettes** | Human figures in dense fog | Separates the formation | Call out names to verify positions |
| **Shifting Terrain** | False paths over real traps | Leading the squad into hazards | Let the most sane player lead |

To survive these illusions, squads must implement a strict reality-check protocol. When encountering an unusual sight or sound, use spatial voice chat to ask simple confirmation questions like "Do you see a red light ahead?" or "Did anyone just blow the horn?" If your teammates answer negatively, ignore the stimulus and stay close to the group.

<Callout type="warning" title="Severe Madness Protocol">
When an explorer's sanity completely fractures, they must cease navigating. The affected player should follow the physical footsteps of a confirmed sane teammate and rely entirely on verbal directions.
</Callout>
