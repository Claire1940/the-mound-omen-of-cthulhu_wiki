<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Madness System: Perception vs. Reality">

The most innovative aspect of the **the mound omen of cthulhu demo** is the Madness mechanic. As the conquistadors push deeper into the cursed territory, their mental state deteriorates. This isn't just a meter on the UI; it actively changes what the player sees and hears. The game begins to "gaslight" the player, making them doubt their surroundings and, most dangerously, their teammates.

This creates a layer of "chaotic and hilarious yet deeply stressful" gameplay that ACE Team has specifically tuned for the 2026 release.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Equipment and Resource Management">

Survival in the **the mound omen of cthulhu demo** requires meticulous management of the limited resources brought from the Spanish fleet. The demo highlights the importance of the shared inventory, where the party must decide who carries the torch, who carries the gunpowder, and who carries the primary melee weapons.

| Item Category | Primary Use | Scarcity |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Firearms** | High-damage ranged combat | **Very Rare** |
| **Melee Weapons** | Quiet, reliable defense | Common |
| **Torches** | Essential for underground navigation | Limited |
| **Sanity Herbs** | Temporarily reduces madness effects | **Rare** |
| **Treasure Bags** | Increases end-game rewards | Variable |

The weapon system is shared across the whole party. If one player dies, their equipment must be recovered by the survivors, or it is lost to the jungle forever. This adds a "corpse run" mechanic that is particularly punishing in the deeper layers of the mound. In the 2026 demo, players will need to master the art of "tactical retreat" to ensure that valuable steel and powder aren't left in the hands of the Great Old Ones.

<Callout type="success" title="Resource Tip">
Prioritize Sanity Herbs over treasure in the early stages of the demo. A sane team with no gold is better than a dead team with pockets full of cursed emeralds.
</Callout>
