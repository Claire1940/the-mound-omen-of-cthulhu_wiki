<Section icon={Activity} title="Sanity and the Madness System">

The most innovative feature of **the mound omen of cthulhu demo ps5** is its approach to psychological horror. There is no sanity bar on your screen. Instead, madness is a private experience. What you see may not be what your teammates see, creating a breakdown in communication that the jungle exploits.

**Common Hallucinations and Distortions:**
- **Bleeding Jungle**: The environment turns deep red, and the ground appears to pulse.
- **False Horns**: You may hear the extraction horn or a teammate's call for help coming from the wrong direction.
- **Teammate Mimicry**: A friendly silhouette might appear in the fog, but it is actually a hostile entity or a pure illusion.
- **Blood Rain**: Localized storms that only affected players can see, obscuring vision and creating false environmental hazards.

<Callout type="success" title="Reality Check Protocol">
When you see something unusual, use spatial voice chat to ask your teammates: "Do you see the red fog ahead?" If they say no, you are experiencing a hallucination. Do not fire your weapon until a "sane" teammate confirms the target.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Monster Bestiary and Combat Tactics">

The jungle is inhabited by entities that are often as confused and corrupted as the explorers themselves. Understanding their behavior is key to preserving your limited ammunition and health.

- **Y'm-bhi Sentries**: These are reanimated guardians. They don't always attack; sometimes they just watch you from the treeline to build stress. Use a crucifix to check their intent.
- **Undead Wanderers**: The most common threat. They are slow but can overwhelm the ox cart if allowed to swarm.
- **Corrupted Explorers**: If a teammate dies and isn't revived, they may return as a hostile NPC that uses your own tactics against you.
- **The Living Jungle**: Not a single monster, but an environmental state where the flora becomes aggressive and visibility drops to near zero.

<Callout type="warning" title="Enemy Special Rules">
Killing a Y'm-bhi is not always permanent. If the "jungle activity" level is high, the forest may reanimate the corpse minutes after you've moved on. Always keep moving after a skirmish.
</Callout>
