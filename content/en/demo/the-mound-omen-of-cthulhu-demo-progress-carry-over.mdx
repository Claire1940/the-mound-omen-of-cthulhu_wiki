<Section icon={Brain} title="Understanding Sanity and Perceived Progress">

In **The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu**, progress isn't just about loot; it's about maintaining your character's mental state. The game features no traditional "Sanity Bar," making it one of the most immersive systems in 2026 gaming.

**Common Hallucinations to Watch For:**
- **False Horn Calls**: Hearing an extraction signal that isn't real.
- **Blood Rain**: Seeing the environment turn red while your teammates see clear skies.
- **Imposter Teammates**: Seeing a fifth "explorer" following your group.
- **Shifting Terrain**: Safe paths appearing as traps, or solid ground appearing as water.

<Callout type="warning" title="Sanity Warning">
If you experience a "Fully Fractured" state, you should stop leading the group. Follow the spatial voice chat of your teammates, as your visual perception can no longer be trusted to navigate back to the galleon.
</Callout>

To combat these effects, squads should use a **Reality-Check Protocol**. If you see something unusual, ask a teammate to confirm the sighting. If they cannot see it, ignore the entity and move toward the ox cart.
