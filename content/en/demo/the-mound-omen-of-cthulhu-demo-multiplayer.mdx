<Section icon={Target} title="Expedition Steps: The Extraction Loop">

Every multiplayer expedition follows a specific sequence. Deviating from this loop—usually due to greed—is the most common cause of failure in **the mound omen of cthulhu demo multiplayer**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select a Captain's Contract">
    Aboard the ship, choose a contract based on your team's skill. Some require specific relics, while others just need a total treasure value. Read the requirements carefully; if you return without the quota, you gain no progression.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Shoreline Deployment">
    Upon landing, the Ox Cart is deployed. This cart is your mobile inventory. It stores your treasure, spare ammunition, and food. One player should always stay within 10 meters of the cart to ensure it keeps moving.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Noise and Light Management">
    As you move into the jungle, walk instead of running. Use the Oil Lamp sparingly in open areas to avoid being spotted from a distance, but keep it bright in ruins to spot floor traps and hidden Y'm-bhi.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Treasure Loading and Tulu-Metal">
    When you find treasure, don't carry it in your hands for long. It slows you down and prevents weapon use. Move it to the Ox Cart immediately. Use the Medallion to ping for high-value Tulu-metal, but remember the ping is audible to enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Extraction Decision">
    Once the contract is met, the environment will likely be "Red" or "Corrupted" due to your presence. Decide as a team: do you push for bonus loot or head back? In multiplayer, if one person dies, they can return as a "Corrupted Explorer" to hunt you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Fort Progression">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Sanity Management and Hallucinations">

The most innovative feature of **the mound omen of cthulhu demo multiplayer** is the individual sanity system. Unlike other horror games, there is no sanity bar. Instead, the game uses "Perception Distortion" where two players looking at the same spot may see different things.

This system is designed to break down team trust. When your sanity drops, the following hallucinations may occur:

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Visual Distortion">
    - **Blood Rain**: The sky turns red and the ground bleeds.
    - **False Silhouettes**: You see a teammate standing in the fog, but the real player is behind you.
    - **Shifting Terrain**: Paths appear open but lead into traps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auditory Deception">
    - **False Horns**: You hear the extraction horn from the wrong direction.
    - **Phantom Voices**: You hear a teammate calling for help through spatial chat when they are silent.
    - **Enemy Whispers**: Undead sounds appear closer than they are.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Identity Crisis">
    - **Monster Masks**: Your teammates' character models transform into monsters.
    - **Panic Firing**: Players often shoot their friends because they look like Y'm-bhi.
    - **Verification**: Always ask "Is that you?" before shooting into fog.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

| Hallucination | Squad Response Protocol |

<Callout type="info" title="Reality Check Tip">
If you suspect you are hallucinating, stand still and look at a teammate. If they aren't reacting to the "threat" you see, it’s likely in your head. Use the spatial voice chat to describe exactly what you see to confirm with the group.
